Rajesh Hamal's biography

Rajesh Hamal is a prominent Nepali actor, producer, and television host. He was born on June 9, 1964, in Tansen, Palpa district of Nepal. Hamal completed his schooling from the prestigious St. Xavier's School in Kathmandu, Nepal. He later earned a bachelor's degree in English literature from Ratna Rajya Laxmi Campus, Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu. Hamal began his career as a television host in 1985 with the show "Kathmandu Today." He then made his acting debut in the Nepali movie "Yug Dekhi Yug Samma" in 1991, which was a massive hit at the box office.


Apart from acting, Hamal has also produced several Nepali movies, including "Hamro Maya Juni Junilai," "Nepathya," and "Nepal Pyaro Chha," among others. In addition to his acting and producing career, Hamal has also been a popular television host, having hosted shows such as "Ko Bancha Crorepati" (Nepali version of "Who Wants to Be a Millionaire") and "Comedy Hostel."


Hamal is also actively involved in social work and has been a goodwill ambassador for the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA) in Nepal since 2010. He is known for his philanthropic work, especially in the areas of education and health. Hamal is considered one of the most successful and influential actors in the Nepali film industry. His contribution to Nepali cinema has earned him the title of "Mahanayak" (Great Actor) in Nepal.
